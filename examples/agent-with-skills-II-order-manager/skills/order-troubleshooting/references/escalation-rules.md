# Escalation Rules

## When to escalate to a human supervisor

Escalate the case if **any** of the following conditions are met:

1. **Suspected fraud**: the order or account shows signs of fraudulent activity.
2. **High-value re-ship**: the order total exceeds $500 and a full re-shipment is required.
3. **Repeat issues**: the customer has had 3 or more issues in the last 30 days (check `issuesLast30Days` from the customer record).
4. **Customer requests supervisor**: the customer explicitly asks to speak with a manager or supervisor.

## How to escalate

1. Summarize the case: order ID, customer, issue classification, and actions already taken.
2. Note which escalation criteria were triggered.
3. Inform the support agent that the case requires supervisor review.
4. Do **not** tell the customer the specific internal reason for escalation (e.g., do not say "your account has too many issues").

## What not to escalate

- Routine refunds within policy
- Simple payment retries
- Standard shipping delays under 7 days
- General order inquiries
